NRF23 Steering

Design, manufacture, test and implement the steering assembly on the NRF23 race car to optimize controlled turning and force reduction on the driver.

Skills: SolidWorks; Design Reviews; Mill, Lathe and CNC manufacturing

Steering Components

  • Ackermann Geometry: Determines how the car turns and therefore determines the forces on the whole car.

  • Steering Rack: Converts the rotational motion of the steering wheel to the lateral motion of the tierods.

  • U-joints: Tranfers rotational motion across a sharp angle.

  • Quick Release: Allows the steering wheel to be quickly attached and removed from the car.

  • Adaptors: Connects the quick release, columns, u-joints, bearings, and steering rack.

  • Columns: Transfers rotational motion of turning to the steering rack.

  • Steering Mount: Holds the bearings and top adaptor and attaches to the from of the car

  • Bearings: Allow the top adaptor to spin inside the steering mount.

Requirements

  • The steering wheel must be mechanically connected to the front wheels.
  • Steering systems must use a rigid mechanical linkage capable of tension and compression loads for operation.
  • The driver must be able to operate the quick disconnect while in the normal driving position with gloves on.
  • Allowable steering system free play is limited to seven degrees total measured at the steering wheel.
  • Etc.

Current Progress

Currently, our Ackermann angles have been calculated, and the purchasing orders for the steering rack, u-joints, quick release and bearings are being processed.

CAD models for the steering columns, adaptors and steering mount have been made, measured in relation to the frame, and are going through FEA simulations.

CADs for the pillow blocks (which attaches the steering rack to the frame) and jigging (which holds pieces in place to be welded) will be made in the coming weeks.

Manufacturing will begin at the beginning of 2023 with the intention of having first drive in early March.
